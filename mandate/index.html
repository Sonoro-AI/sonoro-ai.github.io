Part VII The Mandate

The role, the scope, the architect this platform needs.

The mandate

This is not an app build.
It is the architecture
of a platform company.

i.

AI architecture & orchestration

Design the orchestration layer that routes between LLM, knowledge graph, recommendation engine and approved content library — with bounded outputs, traceable sources and care-grade safety.

ii.

API strategy & integration spine

Architect a single integration spine that composes MPW Academy, TLC, Learn Care Excel, Curam and the ATS into one product surface — with versioning, rate-limiting, and partner-grade SLAs.

iii.

The data model

Design a data model that captures resident preference, menu performance, candidate profile, training record and operator-level governance — and turns it into a defensible asset over time.

iv.

Recommendation logic

Own the algorithms — the Happiness Algorithm, the Marco Standard benchmark, the substitution engine, the training pathway recommender. Each one a research problem with a commercial outcome.

v.

Security & governance

Build for a clinical environment from day one. Role-based permission models, audit logs, PII isolation, hallucination guardrails. Care is regulated. The product is governance.

vi.

Scalable SaaS architecture

Single-home pilot to multi-tenant enterprise, with regional dashboards, cross-portfolio benchmarking and white-label readiness. The architecture must survive a 100× scale event.

vii.

Product roadmap ownership

Co-own the sequencing of phases, the prioritisation of integrations, and the timing of the data flywheel. This is a co-founder mandate, not a contractor mandate.

viii.

A defensible data layer

Within 18 months Sonoro should hold the most comprehensive food-in-care performance dataset in the UK. The architecture must make that asset legally clean, commercially licensable and structurally inseparable from the platform.

The proposition

A greenfield platform mandate, with content, workforce and partnerships already in place.

Most CTO opportunities offer scope or scale, not both. Sonoro offers both. The content is already proprietary. The workforce network is already 140,000 strong. The partner stack is already lined up. What's missing is the architect.
