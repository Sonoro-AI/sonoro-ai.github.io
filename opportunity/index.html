Part II The Opportunity

Why food in care is no longer a back-office cost — it is dignity, memory, and measurable wellbeing.

The opportunity

Food in care is not catering.
It is dignity, memory, and
measurable wellbeing.

The UK adult social care market exceeds £25bn annually. Within it, food sits at the intersection of clinical safety, resident satisfaction, family confidence, regulator scrutiny and operational margin. Today it is run on instinct, spreadsheets and tribal chef knowledge. Sonoro replaces that with infrastructure.

£25bn+UK adult social care marketA category that has out-grown the tools running it.
17,500UK care homesEach one a kitchen, a workforce, a daily decision system.
140kCare workers in the Curam networkAn addressable workforce ready for assessment, training, redeployment.
500+MPW Academy modules ready to licenseA proprietary content layer no competitor can replicate.
i.

Costs are rising. Capability is uneven. Memory is dying with the chef.

Care homes face escalating food costs, inconsistent kitchen capability, and an ageing senior chef cohort whose judgement walks out of the door at retirement. There is no system that captures it, scales it, or transfers it.

Food inflationSkills shortageTacit knowledge loss
ii.

Resident need is complex. Dietary, cultural, clinical, emotional.

A single home may serve residents requiring IDDSI texture modification, allergen exclusions, halal or kosher provision, low-sodium diabetic plans, and culturally specific dishes — all in one service. Today this is held in heads and paper folders.

IDDSIAllergensReligious & culturalClinical nutrition
iii.

Recruitment is broken. The wrong people end up in the wrong kitchens.

A care home kitchen is not a restaurant kitchen. It demands different temperaments, different empathy, different communication. Generic hiring funnels do not screen for it. Sonoro benchmarks every candidate against the Marco Standard for care.

Behavioural fitCare empathyCognitive alignment
iv.

Quality is unevidenced. Regulators ask. Operators guess.

CQC, families, head office and clinical leads all want to know: is the food good? Is it improving? Is it safe? Sonoro turns subjective answers into a continuous, auditable, longitudinal score per dish, per home, per region.
