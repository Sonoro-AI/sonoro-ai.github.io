Part III The Platform

Four engines, one orchestration layer. The product story.

The platform

Four engines. One orchestration layer. A connected operating system for food in care.

Sonoro is not a feature set. It is four product engines that share a single intelligence layer — meaning every interaction in one strengthens the others. A menu adjustment improves training recommendations. A recruitment outcome refines the Marco Standard. A resident's plate-waste signal reweights the algorithm.

a.

Ask Marco

An AI culinary assistant trained on the standards, judgement and editorial voice of Marco Pierre White. Routes questions into practical advice, training modules, recruitment actions or menu interventions.

  • Conversational interface
  • Approved content library
  • Action routing
  • Editorial voice
b.

Menu Intelligence

A guided builder and reviewer that scores every dish against resident preference, IDDSI suitability, allergen profile, cultural fit, nutrition, cost and operational practicality. Suggests improvements or approved alternatives.

  • Seasonal builder
  • Approved-recipe library
  • Nutrition & allergen graph
  • IDDSI texture mapping
c.

Happiness Algorithm

A weighted scoring engine that synthesises survey data, plate-waste, family feedback, staff observation and nutritional measurement into a single dish-level score. Recommendations to chef. Approval to manager.

  • Multi-source signal fusion
  • Per-dish wellbeing index
  • Green / amber / red / blue states
  • Auditable longitudinal trend
d.

Talent Intelligence

A workforce profiling and recruitment layer that benchmarks chefs and care workers against the Marco Standard — measuring technical, behavioural, cognitive and care-specific traits. Plugs into ATS and Curam.

  • Marco Standard scoring
  • ATS API integration
  • Career pathway mapping
  • Training recommendation

a.Ask Marco

The judgement of a three-Michelin-star chef, available to a 6am care home kitchen.

Ask Marco is not generic GPT with a costume. It is a tightly scoped culinary assistant with an editorial voice, an approved content library, and an action layer. It answers the question — and then routes the next step into training, menu, recruitment, or funded learning.

i.

Practical kitchen counsel

Improving a dish, recovering a sauce, adjusting a service, tightening a brigade. Direct, opinionated, useful.

ii.

Texture-modified excellence

IDDSI level guidance, presentation that preserves dignity, taste integrity at every consistency.

iii.

Training recommendations

Routes the user to MPW Academy modules, Learn Care Excel courses, and TLC funded apprenticeship pathways.

iv.

Recruitment & staffing signals

Identifies capability gaps, escalates them, and recommends candidates from the Curam network when appropriate.

v.

Approved document service

Serves only what has been editorially and operationally approved. No hallucination tolerance in a clinical environment.

"How do I improve a puréed chicken dish so it feels dignified and appetising?"

Marco

Right. The first error people make with a purée is treating it as a problem of texture only. It's a problem of respect. The dish must look like a dish, not like a process.

Take your poached chicken — finish it in good butter and chicken stock, then purée with a splash of double cream. Pass through a tamis. Twice. The smoothness is non-negotiable for IDDSI Level 4 — no lumps, no grain, sits on the spoon and holds its shape.

Plate it with intention. A quenelle, not a dollop. A clean band of seasoned mash beside it. A jus reduced and brushed — not flooded. The eye decides whether the resident eats. Always.

b.Menu Intelligence

Every dish, scored. Every alternative, approved. Every change, auditable.

Most kitchens already have a seasonal menu. Sonoro reviews it. For each dish: nutrition, allergens, IDDSI adaptability, cultural fit, cost band, resident feedback, plate-waste signal. If a dish is failing, Sonoro decides whether the answer is execution (fix with Marco) or substitution (suggest an approved alternative with similar nutritional and cost profile).

Operating principle

When a dish underperforms, Sonoro decides whether the issue is execution or fit. Execution routes to Marco. Fit routes to an approved alternative — matched on protein, nutrition, allergens, IDDSI suitability and cost band.

c.The Happiness Algorithm

A single, weighted score that turns resident wellbeing into infrastructure.

Every dish receives a Sonoro score out of 100 — a synthesis of resident preference, actual consumption, nutrition, dietary fit, allergen safety, cultural alignment, cost control, operational practicality, menu balance and emotional familiarity. The algorithm recommends. The chef, manager and clinical lead approve.

Governance principle

The algorithm recommends. The chef, manager or clinical lead approves. No automated change is ever served to a resident without human sign-off.

d.Talent Intelligence

The Marco Standard — a four-dimension benchmark for the people who feed the most vulnerable.

Restaurant hiring filters for technique. Care kitchen hiring needs more. Sonoro benchmarks every chef and care-kitchen candidate against four dimensions — technical, behavioural, cognitive, care-specific — and feeds the result into the operator's ATS. The right people. The right kitchens. The right reasons.

a.

Technical

The craft. Measurable, observable, drillable.

  • Cooking fundamentals
  • Timing & brigade flow
  • Seasoning judgement
  • Consistency under volume
  • Texture-modified adaptation
  • Plate presentation
b.

Behavioural

The temperament. Non-negotiable in service.

  • Discipline
  • Calm under pressure
  • Pride in the plate
  • Resilience across the shift
  • Attention to detail
  • Personal standards
c.

Cognitive

The thinking. Applied judgement, not raw IQ.

  • Forward planning
  • Service-time judgement
  • Adaptability
  • Problem solving
  • Situational awareness
  • Resource economy
d.

Care-specific

The heart. The reason a kitchen exists in a care home.

  • Empathy
  • Dignity-first thinking
  • Resident-centred approach
  • Communication with vulnerability
  • Patience
  • Understanding of decline
Integration spine

The CTO's ATS provides video interview, emotional-intelligence ranking and cognitive alignment. Sonoro consumes those signals through a single API and projects every candidate against the Marco Standard for the role they are applying for.
